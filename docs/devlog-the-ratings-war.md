# THE RATINGS WAR UPDATE — everything got sharper, meaner, and better lit

*The network apologises for the interruption. The interruption is permanent.*

This is the biggest update since SLASH TV first went on air. Play it free in

## The cast got a makeover

Zombies aren't coloured blobs anymore. Every one of them now has arms that
reach for you, a head that leans into the chase, and a walk you can read at a
glance — sprinters pump their arms like they're late for something, brutes
haul shoulder slabs, boomers waddle behind a bloated belly, stalkers trail
ghost-wisps as they fade. The bosses got the same treatment: **The Producer**
now struts the stage in his gold suit and thrusts a shotgun-mic at you while
he tracks your every move, and **The Executive** takes your call — and your
hearts — with a phone welded to his ear.

Press **C** on the title screen for **TONIGHT'S CAST**: every monster, drawn
live by the game itself, with the announcer's honest assessment of each one.

## Two new contestants (they're not on your side)

- **THE SPITTER** — the show's first ranged zombie. It stops at a distance,
  bulges its jaw, and lobs bile at you. Walking backwards forever is no
  longer a strategy. Sidestep or close in.
- **THE GUSHER** — a bigger, meaner spitter that hoses a **three-glob fan**.
  One sidestep is not enough. Waits for you in Episode 3 and deep Endless.
- **ELITES** — any zombie can now come through the door wearing a gold ring:
  triple health, triple score, and the audience *always* throws a gift where
  one falls.

## Boss fights got a beginning, a middle, and an end

Bosses now make an entrance — descending onto their mark, harmless for a
beat, opening with a taunt (*"I MADE THIS SHOW. I CAN UNMAKE YOU."*) — and
they make an **exit**: at zero health every zombie on set drops dead with
them, the star detonates in stages for three full seconds, and a single
glowing EXIT opens that you walk through like the credits are rolling.

And when you beat an episode, **the run keeps going**. Score screen, press
fire, straight into the next episode with your money, kills, and accuracy
carried over. Three episodes, one run, one final number.

## The multiplier is off the leash

No more x9 ceiling. The multiplier climbs one step per 8 chained kills for
as long as you stay untouched — x12, x15, x20 if you're good enough. One
hit still halves it. The greedier the streak, the worse the fall.

## The studio spent money on itself

- **Followspots on all four corners** now track the contestants across the
  floor, operated by someone slightly too slow — the pool where all four
  beams cross is always you.
- Every room gets a **broadcast lower-third** on entry: room name, red
  accent, blinking LIVE bug.
- New maps for all three episodes — proper branching lattices with up to
  five routes to the boss, your call at every door.
- A full synthesized **sound pass**: kills pitch by body size, the
  multiplier climbs audibly, and bosses get a dread sting at half health.

## Mobile got real

- **Add to Home Screen** and SLASH TV now runs as an app: true fullscreen,
  no browser bar, landscape-locked, and **playable offline** after one visit.
- A visible pause button that isn't hiding under the score, and **tap
  anywhere to resume**.
- Tap targets on the title screen actually work now (CAM-BOT co-op, tonight's
  Syndication, the cast page — all one tap).

## Also in this broadcast

- Two-player co-op spelled out on the title screen: **B** for a local
  CAM-BOT AI partner, **H** to host online and send a friend the link.
- Movement re-tuned: slower, heavier, and stopping actually stops.
- Rockets detonate on proximity with a proper shockwave; the flamethrower
  looks like a flamethrower.
- Power-up drops rebalanced (no more three pairs of boots at once).
- One genuine crash bug fixed (rockets vs boomer clusters — you're welcome).
